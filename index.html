The Story

Where Village Life Blooms

Nestled in the heart of Pustaka Gramam — Kerala's first and India's second "Book Village" — KoKo is more than just a farm; it is a bridge to our roots.

Kochattan, a symbol of the older generation who lives in harmony with nature, is your guide to this timeless world. He is a storehouse of local knowledge, traditions, and the simple joys of village life. Through his eyes, you'll see a world where every tree has a story and every path leads to a memory.

Our vision is to recreate the authentic soul of a Kerala village: a mud hut with a thatched roof, cow-dung-polished floors, and a kitchen echoing with the rhythmic sounds of the attukallu and arakallu. It's a place where children can rediscover traditional games and adults can find a quiet pause from the rush of modern life.

Landmarks & Initiatives

Where Every Corner Has a Story

From silent meditation spaces to gathering hubs, KoKo is a collection of thoughtfully designed "spots" that invite you to pause and reconnect.

ചെമ്മാനപ്പടി

Chemmanappadi

Our sunset and sunrise seating space — a quiet culvert designed for golden hour reflections.

പാടശാല

Padasala

An elevated 'Erumadam' (peace hut) by the pond and paddy fields. A place to sing memories to the breeze.

തമ്പ്

Thambu

Not just a shelter, but an emotion. A bamboo haven where every node holds a village story.

തളം

Thalam

A gentle open space woven with green. A silent invitation to pause, reflect, and reconnect with nature.

തപോവനം

Tapovan

Our nature-friendly meditation space. Step away from the noise and find your inner stillness.

തവളക്കുളം

Frog Pond

A pond full of purpose. A thriving ecosystem that reminds us of nature's delicate balance.

ഈശ്വരപ്പൊന്ന്

Eeshwarapponnu

Reviving our roots through the story of "Paddy in a Pot" — a unique initiative to bring back lost harvests.

കട്ട സെൽഫി

Katta Selfie

A unique village-style photo spot where the backdrop is the raw, unpolished beauty of the farm.

The Village Feel

Rooted in Kerala Tradition

KoKo is not just a farm — it's a living celebration of Kerala's village culture, seasons, and soul.

Onam Festivities

Celebrate the harvest festival with a grand sadhya on banana leaves, pookalam designs, Thiruvathira, and traditional games in the open village air.

Vishu & New Beginnings

Welcome the Malayalam New Year with a Vishukkani — the auspicious first sight of gold, fruits, and flowers arranged at dawn on the farm.

Traditional Games

Kalari warm-ups, kambadakali, uriyadi, and tug-of-war under the shade of old mango trees. Village games the whole family can join.

Local Food & Flavours

Kanji with pappadom, chakka curry, nadan kozhi (chicken) fry, puttu-kadala, and fresh toddy coconut served straight from the farm — real Kerala food.
