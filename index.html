About the Farm

A Living Piece of Kerala Village Life

Nestled in the lush folds of Pustaka Gramam — "the village of books" — just a short drive from Kottarakara town, KoKo is a working farm rooted in the rhythms of traditional Kerala agriculture.

Here, banana groves rustle in the afternoon breeze, pepper vines climb ancient trees, and papaya gardens glow golden at harvest time. Our dream is simple: to invite you to slow down, get your feet muddy, and experience the warmth of village life — the way it has always been.

Kochattan — our resident village elder and your guide — knows every corner of this land and every story it holds. He can't wait to show you around.

What to Expect

Experiences That Stay With You

Every visit to KoKo is an invitation to live the village — not just visit it. Here's a glimpse of what Kochattan has planned for you.

Village Walks

Wander through coconut groves, banana gardens, and winding farm paths with Kochattan as guide.

Farm-to-Table Cooking

Cook a Kerala meal using ingredients picked fresh from the farm — banana, pepper, coconut, and more.

Craft Workshops

Coir weaving, clay pottery, leaf-art, and traditional Kerala crafts — hands-on village skills.

Nature Trails

Wander along the Padavela pond, through spice gardens, and into the quiet heart of Pustaka Gramam.

Seasonal Harvests

Join banana picking, papaya harvest, pepper collection, and experience the joy of farm bounty.

Cycling Tours

Pedal along with Kochattan on a guided cycle tour through village lanes and farm boundaries.

Cultural Evenings

Onam sadhya, Vishu celebrations, traditional games, folk music, and starlit storytelling sessions.

Bullock Cart Rides

Rumble through the farm on a traditional bullock cart — a ride that takes you back in time.

The Village Feel

Rooted in Kerala Tradition

KoKo is not just a farm — it's a living celebration of Kerala's village culture, seasons, and soul.

🌸

Onam Festivities

Celebrate the harvest festival with a grand sadhya on banana leaves, pookalam designs, Thiruvathira, and traditional games in the open village air.

☀️

Vishu & New Beginnings

Welcome the Malayalam New Year with a Vishukkani — the auspicious first sight of gold, fruits, and flowers arranged at dawn on the farm.

🎯

Traditional Games

Kalari warm-ups, kambadakali, uriyadi, and tug-of-war under the shade of old mango trees. Village games the whole family can join.

🍃

Local Food & Flavours

Kanji with pappadom, chakka curry, nadan beef fry, puttu-kadala, and fresh toddy coconut served straight from the farm — real Kerala food.
